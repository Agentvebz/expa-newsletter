9 STORIES · LIVE This week in AI, tap to read Claude #1 · KPMG · Karpathy · $965B · Pharma · Opus 4.8 · +3 ⌄
01
🏆 Claude just overtook OpenAI as the #1 business AI
A year ago, OpenAI owned the enterprise: 32% of U.S. business adoption versus Anthropic's 8%. The gap looked uncatchable.
Per Ramp's May 2026 AI Index, tracking actual corporate card spend across thousands of companies, Anthropic now sits at 34.4%, OpenAI at 32.3%. First time ever in the lead.

Why it matters for us: we're already on the platform that just became the enterprise leader. Not nothing.
02
🤝 KPMG rolled Claude out to 276,000 people
Anthropic and KPMG announced a Claude deployment across KPMG's entire global workforce and into their client-facing Digital Gateway platform. When a Big Four consultancy goes all-in on Claude for regulated client work, the pharma-shaped enterprise wave isn't far behind.
03
🧠 Karpathy joined Anthropic
Andrej Karpathy, ex-OpenAI, ex-Tesla, arguably AI's most influential researcher, has officially joined Anthropic for frontier R&D. Roughly the equivalent of Ronaldo signing for Manchester United. Talent follows momentum.
04
💰 Anthropic is now the most valuable private AI company
Anthropic closed a $65B round at a $965B valuation, overtaking OpenAI (~$852B) to become the most valuable private AI company in the world.
Annualized revenue is tracking past $45B, roughly 5× where it sat at the end of 2024. Momentum, in one number.

Why it matters for us: the platform several of our tools run on just became the best-capitalized one in the field.
05
🧬 Anthropic is leaning hard into life sciences
Anthropic acquired AI-for-biology firm Coefficient Bio (~$400M), deepening its Claude for Life Sciences push. Pharma names already piloting Claude across R&D and ops include Sanofi, Novo Nordisk, AbbVie, and Genmab.
Why it matters for us: the same model is being purpose-built for regulated pharma workflows, closer to home than generic enterprise AI.
06
⚙️ Claude Opus 4.8 shipped, with effort controls
Anthropic released Claude Opus 4.8, adding "effort controls" (dial reasoning depth up or down per task) and a roughly 3× cheaper fast mode. Worth knowing, since it's the model behind this newsletter and several of our team tools.
07
🧩 Microsoft is bundling Copilot into one "super app"
Microsoft is reportedly merging GitHub Copilot, Copilot Chat, and a new agentic "Autopilot" feature into a single app, targeting a summer launch, a push to lift adoption past the under-4.5% of its 450M Microsoft 365 users who currently touch any Copilot feature.
The read: even the biggest vendors are still fighting the last-mile adoption problem. Distribution > features.
08
🤔 Developers now refuse to code without AI
Engineers won't give up AI assistants even briefly for research, despite a 2025 METR study that found AI slowed experienced devs by ~19% on familiar codebases. One company reportedly burned its entire 2026 AI budget by April.
For an ML team: a clean reminder that perceived productivity and measured productivity aren't the same thing, worth measuring our own.
09
🔎 AI search is reshuffling the deck
After Google replaced its classic results page with an AI-first conversational engine, privacy-search rival DuckDuckGo saw US app installs jump roughly 30% in a week. The takeaway for our Search/GEO work: how answers get surfaced is changing fast.
10
🎯 Your move next: NOVA is live
◆ LILLY · AI UPSKILLING
While Claude was busy winning enterprise, Lilly's own NOVA AI Upskilling Program went live earlier this month. It pairs personalized AI learning paths with Workera, a skill-assessment platform that maps your current capabilities against where you want to be, all SSO-enabled, zero friction to start.
